Kerala Style Chicken Biriyani

Biriyani or Biryani – the rich festive food made with rice, meat, vegetables, eggs, fish and spices, has become a common Indian household dish. With most of the ingredients available readily, making Biryani is no longer a difficult task. I remember my childhood days when Briyani was made by my mother only on very special occasions, probably twice or thrice in a year. We used to enjoy every bit of it and looked forward for the next special occasion. Now it is made almost every week or atleast once in a month by most of us. 🙂

The other day I was reading the difference between Biryani and Pulao. While Pulav is made by cooking all the ingredients together, rice is cooked separately and mixed with meat or vegetables for Biryani. Well that was a revelation. There are so many regional variations for Biryani. Some of the well known ones are Hyderabadi Biryani, Sindhi Biryani, Awadhi Biryani, Malabar or Kozhikode Biriyani and so on.

I usually make Kerala Style Chicken Biriyani but I never had the patience to post the recipe. After receiving several requests from my readers, well finally, here is my recipe for Chicken Biriyani. Kerala Style Biriyani is aromatic and flavorful. The addition of Fennel Seeds (Perum Jeerakam) and tomatoes make Kerala Style Biryani taste out of this world. Additionally, Coconut Milk is often used in South Indian Biriyani and the taste gets even better. One ingredient which is sometimes left out while making Kerala Biriyani is saffron.

This is the recipe for Layered Chicken Biryani which is baked in an oven. Though I have tried making one pot Biriyanis, it has never come out well.

Serves: 6

Ingredients for the Rice
  • Long grain Basmati rice – 2 cups
  • Oil – 1 tsp
  • Biriyani Masala Powder – 1/2 tsp
  • Water – as required
  • Salt – to taste
Ingredients for Garnishing
  • Thinly Sliced Red Onion – 1 big
  • Cashew Halves – 1/4 cup
  • Raisins – 1/4 cup
  • Almonds – 1/4 cup (optional)
  • Sugar – 1/2 tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Oil/Ghee – 1 to 2 tbsp
Ingredients for the Biriyani Masala Gravy
  • Thinly sliced Red Onions – 2 large
  • Diced Tomatoes – 2 large
  • Potato (peeled and diced) – 1 big (optional)
  • Slit Green Chillies – 4
  • Cinnamon – 1″ piece
  • Cloves – 4
  • Fennel Seeds – 1/2 tsp
  • Bay leaves – 2 to 4
  • Chopped Cilantro (Malliyilla) – 1/2 cup
  • Chopped Mint leaves (Pudhina) – 1/2 cup
  • Dried Fenugreek Leaves (Methi/Uluva) – A few
  • Fresh Ginger Garlic Paste – 2 tbsp
  • Turmeric Powder – 1/4 tsp
  • Kashmiri Chilly Powder – 2 tsp
  • Chicken Masala Powder – 2 tbsp
  • Home Made Biriyani Masala Powder – 1 to 2 tsp
  • Yogurt – 1/2 Cup
  • Lemon Juice – 2 tbsp
  • Salt- to taste
  • Ghee/Cooking oil – 2 tbsp
Ingredients for Marinating the Chicken
  • Whole Chicken (medium sized pieces) – 1 lb
  • Lemon Juice – 1 tbsp
  • Turmeric Powder – 1/4 tsp
  • Chicken Masala Powder – 1 tbsp
  • Kashmiri Chilly Powder – 1 tsp
  • Pepper Powder – 1/2 tsp
  • Coriander Powder – 1 tbsp
  • Fresh Ginger Garlic Paste – 1 tbsp
  • Salt – to taste
Preparation Method
  1. Soak Basmathi rice in water for around 20-30 minutes.
  2. Wash and clean the chicken pieces thoroughly and soak it in water along with 1 tbsp lemon juice for around 5 minutes. Drain and keep aside.
  3. Make a marinade using the above ingredients and rub it nicely on the chicken pieces. Keep it aside for atleast 30 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, heat 1 tbsp oil/ghee in a nonstick pan and fry cashews, almonds and raisins until the cashews and almonds are lightly browned and the raisins become plump.
  5. To the same pan, add 1 more tbsp of oil/ghee. Throw in the thinly sliced onions for garnishing and saute until caramelized (15-20 mins). Add a pinch of salt and 1/2 tsp of sugar.
  6. Boil rice in a big pan with salt, 1/2 tsp biryani masala, and 1 tsp oil until 3/4 cooked (8-10 mins). Drain and keep aside.
  7. Heat 2 tbsp oil/ghee in a wok. Throw in fennel seeds, cinnamon, bay leaves and cloves. Saute for 2 minutes.
  8. Add onions, green chillies, fenugreek leaves, cilantro, and mint. Saute till onions brown.
  9. Add ginger garlic paste, then chopped tomatoes. Cook until pulpy.
  10. Reduce heat; add biriyani masala, chicken masala, kashmiri chilly powder, turmeric, and salt. Saute for a minute.
  11. Add yogurt blended with water. Simmer.
  12. Add marinated chicken and potatoes. Cover and cook on medium heat for 20 minutes.
  13. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a baking dish.
  14. Layer the rice and chicken gravy alternatively. Garnish with fried onions, cashews, and raisins.
  15. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Let it cool before mixing gently.
Note
  • You can add coconut milk instead of yogurt for a better flavor.
  • If you do not have an oven, you can use the stove top "Dum" method by sealing the lid with dough and cooking on very low heat over indirect heat (like placing the pan on a Tawa).
